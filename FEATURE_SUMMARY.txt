OLD APPROACH (Still has interruption):
  stop_stream()  ❌ Disconnects immediately
        ↓
  resolution = (1920, 1080)
        ↓
  start_stream()  ⏳ Wait 1-5 seconds
        ↓
  [BLACK SCREEN]

NEW APPROACH (Smooth transition):
  change_resolution_smooth()  ✅ Keeps old stream running
        ↓
  [Old stream: 640x360 continues]
  [New stream: 1920x1080 loads in background]
        ↓
  [When new stream ready]
        ↓
  [Switch to new stream, clean up old]
        ↓
  [CONTINUOUS VIDEO - NO INTERRUPTION]

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Resolution Transitions:
  • 640x360  ↔  1920x1080 (fullscreen toggle)
  • Transition time: 1-5 seconds (typical)
  • Extensible to other resolutions

Resource Usage During Transition:
  • Memory: Temporary +5-20MB (2x streams)
  • Bandwidth: Temporary 2x (both streams)
  • CPU: +10-20% additional
  • Duration: Only during 1-5 second transition

After Transition:
  • All resources return to normal
  • Single stream actively running


METHOD SIGNATURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

class CameraInstance:
    def change_resolution_smooth(
        self, 
        new_resolution: Tuple[int, int], 
        on_ready_callback=None
    ) -> None:
        """
        Change camera resolution smoothly without disconnecting.
        
        Keeps current low-resolution stream running while starting
        high-resolution stream in parallel. Switches when new stream
        is ready, then cleans up old stream.
        
        Args:
            new_resolution: Target resolution (width, height)
            on_ready_callback: Optional callback when ready
        """
